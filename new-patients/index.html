Good News

You Don't Need a Referral in Ohio

Ohio is a direct-access state. You can start physical therapy without a physician's prescription — which means you don't need to sit on a months-long wait to see a specialist first.

A few insurance plans still require a referral before they'll cover treatment. We'll call your insurance and tell you before your first visit. No surprises.

If you've already seen a physician and have imaging (MRI, X-ray) or an order, bring it along — it's useful, just not required.

What to Expect

A Full Hour, Start to Finish

Every evaluation is 60 minutes with a licensed PT. Here's how that hour is typically spent.

1

Check in & brief intake (5 min)

You'll meet your therapist, confirm your paperwork, and we'll ask you to describe — in your own words — what brought you in.

2

Conversation & history (15 min)

We ask the right questions: what it feels like, what makes it better or worse, what you've tried, and what your goals actually are. This part matters more than people realize.

3

Hands-on assessment (20 min)

Movement screens, strength testing, and specific orthopedic tests. We figure out what's actually driving the problem — not just the site of pain.

4

Explanation & early treatment (15 min)

We walk you through findings in plain English, answer every question, and start treatment so you leave feeling different, not just informed.

5

Plan, home program, next steps (5 min)

You leave with a written home program, a realistic timeline, and a clear follow-up cadence. No guessing.

Before Your Visit

What to Bring

We keep the intake short so you can spend the hour working on you — not on paperwork.

Bring with you

Photo ID
Insurance card (or a photo of the front & back)
Any imaging reports (MRI, X-ray, ultrasound) or recent physician notes
Comfortable clothing (shorts & t-shirt are ideal; sneakers for knee/hip/ankle)
Golf? Bring your own clubs if you're booking a TPI screen.
Questions — write them down so nothing gets forgotten.
