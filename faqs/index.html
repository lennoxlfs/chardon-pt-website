The Rest of Your Questions

Answers, in order of how often we get them.

Do you take insurance?

We're an out-of-network, cash-based practice. We don't bill insurance directly — you pay at the time of visit, and we provide a superbill you can submit to your insurance for potential out-of-network reimbursement.

Most patients find that the value of full-hour, 1-on-1 care with a licensed DPT far exceeds the cost of shorter, in-network visits where you share a therapist with two or three others.

Why cash-based?

Insurance dictates how long visits are, what we can treat, and how many sessions you get. The result at most clinics: 15–20 minutes with a therapist and the rest of the hour with an aide. We stepped outside that system so every patient gets the full hour with a licensed DPT — faster progress, fewer total visits, better outcomes.

How much does it cost?

Pricing is transparent and fair. Call (440) 299-7171 or email team@chardonpt.com for current rates based on service — standard PT, dry needling, BFR, or golf performance. HSA/FSA accepted. Superbills can lower your out-of-pocket cost via out-of-network reimbursement.

Do I need a physician's referral?

Ohio has direct access — you can see a physical therapist without a referral for most conditions. If we find something during your evaluation that warrants imaging or a medical consult, we'll coordinate with your primary care provider or orthopedist.

What should I expect at my first visit?

A full 60 minutes with your therapist. We'll:

  • Talk through your history, goals, and what's been tried before
  • Perform a thorough movement and orthopedic evaluation
  • Begin treatment the same day — often manual therapy, dry needling (if indicated), and individualized exercises
  • Send you home with a clear plan and a small home program to keep momentum

No waiting rooms full of patients. No switching between aides. Just you and your therapist.

How many visits will I need?

It depends, but most patients resolve their primary concern in 4–8 visits — far fewer than the 20–30 often prescribed at in-network clinics. Our model is built around fewer, higher-quality sessions.

What's dry needling, and does it hurt?

Dry needling uses thin filament needles to target trigger points, fascia, and muscle tissue — it's different from acupuncture in both technique and goal. Most patients describe it as a deep ache, sometimes a brief twitch, not sharp pain.

Multiple clinicians on our team hold advanced dry-needling certifications (CIDN and Cert. DN through AAMT) — among the more rigorous credentials in the field.

Can I use my HSA or FSA?

Yes. Physical therapy is a qualified medical expense under HSA/FSA rules. We accept HSA/FSA cards and provide receipts with all the coding needed for reimbursement.

What should I wear?

Comfortable, movement-friendly clothing. Gym clothes work great. If we're treating a specific joint, loose or layered clothing that allows access helps.

Do you treat kids and teens?

Yes. We see athletes from middle school through college, as well as younger patients with orthopedic or movement concerns. A parent or guardian attends the first visit for patients under 18.

Can I book online?

We prefer a quick phone call so we can match you with the right therapist and the right service from the start. Call (440) 299-7171 or send a message and we'll call you back the same business day.

Still have questions?

Call or email — we'd rather talk it through than send you in circles online.
