Concussions

By David Nelson, PT, DPT · November 22, 2022

Concussions. Let's cut through the gray area in athletics and stop "guessing" or "assuming" what to do. In this conversation we walk through what happens on the field, what the next 24 hours should look like, who you should actually be talking to, and what real treatment — not rest-on-the-couch treatment — looks like.

On-field injury screen (0:00 – 2:50)

The sideline moment is where most mistakes happen. The athlete wants to go back in. The coach wants the player back in. The parent is scared. Someone has to be the adult who asks the uncomfortable questions and runs a proper on-field screen. We break down what a real screen looks like: balance, eye tracking, memory, symptom provocation. Two minutes of honest assessment beats two months of a missed concussion.

The first 24 hours after a suspected concussion (2:50 – 6:10)

You don't put them in a dark room for three days anymore. That's outdated. Current evidence supports relative rest for a short window, then a graded, monitored return to light activity. What you don't do: drive them home alone, let them crush caffeine, or send them back to practice "just to see." We cover what the first 24 hours should actually look like and the red flags that mean ER — not ice pack.

Who to talk to (6:15 – 7:15)

Not every healthcare provider is up to date on current concussion care, and that's a problem. We lay out who you should be calling first, who can run proper baseline testing, and which providers in Northeast Ohio are trained specifically in concussion rehab. Getting to the right person in week one saves months of post-concussion symptoms.

Treatment — what actually works (7:26 – 10:00)

Treatment has come a long way. Vestibular rehab, ocular motor training, graded aerobic exertion, and neck-focused manual therapy are all evidence-supported pieces of modern concussion care. We walk through what sessions look like, what to expect from progress, and how to know you're getting real treatment vs. just being told to "wait it out."

Prevention & resources

While we can't always prevent a concussion, we CAN take steps to optimize our care and diagnosis if one happens. Baseline testing (like the ImPACT screen) and proper safety equipment for contact sports both move the needle. A short list of trusted resources:

  • Courtney Crawford, PT at Chardon Performance Therapy — (440) 299-7171
  • UH Concussion Management Program
  • Cleveland Clinic Concussion Services
  • ImPACT Screen baseline testing

If you're a parent, coach, or athlete and something doesn't feel right after a hit — don't wait. Get it looked at by someone who treats concussions regularly. The earlier we intervene, the cleaner the recovery.
