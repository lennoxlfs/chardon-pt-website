Caring for Our Community This Season: Supporting Local Mini Food Pantries

By Angelia Smith · December 16, 2025

At Chardon Performance Therapy, we believe caring for people extends far beyond the walls of the clinic. True health includes feeling supported, seen, and secure — especially during the holiday season, when real life doesn't pause for anyone.

This year, we're proud to continue our community outreach by helping fill local mini food pantries in Chardon. From Thanksgiving through Christmas, our goal is simple: make sure no one in our community goes hungry.

Why mini food pantries matter

Local mini food pantries provide immediate, no-barrier access to essentials for individuals and families who need support. No applications. No questions. No red tape. Just neighbors helping neighbors. Every donation we receive goes directly into these pantries, so items are on the shelves right away for people who need them most.

What you can donate

We're currently accepting a wide range of items that make a real difference:

  • Non-perishable food items — canned goods, peanut butter, shelf-stable meals
  • "Just add water" baking mixes — pancake mix, cornbread, muffins
  • Oatmeal, rice, pasta, dried beans, and basic staples
  • Hygiene supplies — toothpaste, soap, deodorant, feminine products
  • Cleaning supplies — all-purpose cleaner, dish soap, laundry detergent
  • Baby supplies — diapers, wipes, formula
  • Household essentials — toilet paper, paper towels, trash bags

These items are often just as critical as food — and often harder for families to access consistently.

How to get involved

If you're looking for a way to give back this season, we'd love to help you plug in.

Text Chardon Performance Therapy at (440) 299-7171 — we'll share drop-off options, current needs, and where your donation is going. Whether it's one item or a full bag, your generosity helps build a stronger, healthier Chardon — one family at a time.

Thank you for caring

Community isn't built by one person or one business. It's built when people keep showing up for each other — consistently, quietly, and with intention. Thank you for helping us care for all of Chardon this holiday season.

Give back

Drop off a donation — we'll take care of the rest

Text (440) 299-7171 or stop by the clinic. Every item goes straight into a local Chardon mini food pantry.
