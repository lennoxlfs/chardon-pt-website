About Our Practice

Physical Therapy Built Around You

At Chardon Performance Therapy, we take a different approach. Every session is 1-on-1 with your therapist — no aides, no rotating staff, no rushed appointments. We spend the time to understand your entire health picture and build a custom care plan that actually works.

Founded in 2020 by Dave Nelson, PT, DPT, our practice was built on a simple belief: you deserve better care than the traditional physical therapy model delivers. Whether you’re an athlete recovering from injury, a weekend warrior managing chronic pain, or someone who just wants to move without limitations — we’re here for you.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

No referral is needed. In Ohio, you have direct access to physical therapy. You can schedule your first appointment directly with us — no doctor’s referral required. We’ll handle any coordination with your physician if needed.

Every session is truly 1-on-1 with your physical therapist. No aides, no technicians, no being shared between multiple patients. We spend the full session time with you, discussing your complete health picture and building a plan customized to your specific goals and lifestyle.

We treat a wide range of conditions including lower back pain, sciatica, neck pain, shoulder pain, knee pain, TMJ, migraines, headaches, sports injuries, post-surgical rehabilitation, and general mobility limitations. If you’re unsure whether we can help, give us a call and we’ll let you know.

We work with most major insurance providers. Contact us with your insurance information and we’ll verify your coverage and benefits before your first visit so there are no surprises.

Your first visit includes a thorough evaluation where we discuss your health history, lifestyle, goals, and current limitations. We’ll perform a physical assessment to identify the root cause of your issue, then create a custom treatment plan. Most patients also receive hands-on treatment during the first session.

Dry needling is an evidence-based technique where thin filament needles are inserted into trigger points (muscle knots) to release tension, reduce pain, and promote healing. Both of our therapists are Certified in Integrated Dry Needling (CIDN). It’s not acupuncture — it’s based on modern neuroanatomy and is highly effective for muscle pain and dysfunction.
