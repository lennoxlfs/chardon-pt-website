Your swing is a mirror of your body. We use the Titleist Performance Institute (TPI) framework to find the exact physical limitations capping your distance and causing your pain — then rebuild them, one piece at a time.

Why TPI

The Body-Swing Connection

TPI — the Titleist Performance Institute — is the most respected framework in golf medicine and golf fitness. TPI's massive data set has mapped the 13 most common swing faults to their underlying physical causes: limited hip internal rotation, poor thoracic mobility, weak glutes, restricted wrists, imbalanced rotary power.

In Geauga County, truly TPI-certified medical providers are rare. Most instructors teach the swing; we fix the body that swings. Pain goes down. Distance goes up. And the swing change your coach has been asking for actually sticks — because your body can finally do it.

Every golfer — weekend hacker to scratch — benefits from a TPI screen. It takes 20 minutes and usually unlocks changes a golfer has chased for years.

How Golf Therapy Works

A 3-Step Path to a Better Swing

1
TPI physical screen (Level 1)
A 20-minute, 16-point mobility and stability screen. We identify the exact physical limitations that are showing up in your swing — from wrist hinge to hip rotation to thoracic extension.
2
Body-to-swing correlation
We connect each screen finding to specific swing characteristics. If you lose posture in the downswing or slide instead of rotate, the reason is almost always in your screen.
3
Rebuild the body, groove the swing
Manual therapy, dry needling, and a highly specific corrective program — so your body can finally do what your swing coach has been asking for. Most golfers see meaningful change in 4–6 visits.
