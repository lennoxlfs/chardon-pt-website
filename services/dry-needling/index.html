Dry Needling in Chardon, OH

Multiple clinicians on our team hold advanced dry-needling certifications — CIDN (Dave, Courtney) and AAMT Cert. DN (Megan). It's a western, evidence-based way to release stubborn trigger points, calm overactive nerves, and unlock movement that hasn't responded to anything else.

What Dry Needling Is

A Tool That Cuts Through Stubborn Pain

Dry needling uses a thin filament needle — the same kind used in acupuncture, but with a completely different rationale — to target myofascial trigger points and tight bands of muscle. The needle creates a tiny mechanical and neurophysiological change that lets the muscle finally let go.

It's not magic and it's not for everything. But for the right patient — chronic neck pain, tension headaches, glute pain that won't quit, plantar fasciitis, jaw pain — it can do in 1–2 sessions what months of stretching couldn't.

Unlike most clinics where one therapist might be certified, we have multiple clinicians with advanced dry-needling credentials — Dave and Courtney are CIDN; Megan is AAMT Cert. DN — so you get expert needling regardless of who you see.

How a Session Works

What to Expect in Your First Visit

1
Movement & symptom assessment
We test what hurts, what's tight, and what's referred — to confirm whether trigger-point therapy is the right move and which muscles are driving your pain.
2
Targeted needling
Single-use sterile filament needles are inserted into the specific trigger points. You may feel a brief deep ache or twitch — that's the muscle releasing.
3
Movement reset
We immediately re-test the painful or restricted movement and add corrective exercise so the change actually sticks beyond the table.
4
Home plan
A short, specific home program — usually 3–5 minutes a day — to maintain the release and address whatever was causing the muscle to tighten in the first place.
