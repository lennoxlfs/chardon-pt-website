Our Framework

The Life Performance Method.

Pain to performance in three clear phases. Every patient, every case. No guesswork, no "let's see how it goes" — a repeatable path from where you are now to where you want to be.

Why This Exists

Most PT ends too early.

You get to the point where you're "mostly better" — the pain is manageable, you're moving again — and insurance cuts you off. Six months later, you're right back where you started.

The Life Performance Method was built to fix that. It takes you past "mostly better" to actually strong, resilient, and able to handle your life without flaring up again. That's what you're paying for when you come here — not a visit count, but a path to the finish.

01

Pain Management

Get out of pain. Restore range of motion. Create the conditions for real rehab.

Phase 1

Pain Management — get the fire out.

You can't strengthen angry tissue. The first job is to calm things down so we can actually do the work that matters.

This phase uses manual therapy, dry needling (when indicated), targeted mobility work, and short-loop loading to reduce pain and restore the baseline range of motion you need for phase two.

What we use

Manual therapy — joint mobilization and soft-tissue work
Dry needling (CIDN) for stubborn trigger points
Targeted mobility work to restore ROM
Pain-science education — understand what's actually going on
You'll know you're ready for phase 2 when pain is down to a 2/10 or lower, you've got workable range of motion, and the movement triggers you were avoiding feel approachable again.
Phase 2

We Move You — guided strength & control.

Now we get to the work that actually changes your tissue long-term. Strengthening, motor control, and movement retraining — every rep coached by your therapist.

This is where most clinics hand you a sheet of exercises and disappear into another room. We don't. Every set is observed, every rep is loaded at the right dose for your tissue right now, and the plan progresses every visit based on what your body's telling us.

What this looks like

Progressive strength training — correct load, correct dose
Motor control work — the right muscle, the right timing
Blood flow restriction (when low-load is called for)
Movement retraining for sport-, job-, or life-specific tasks
You'll know you're ready for phase 3 when you can load the tissue at near-normal demand, your mechanics hold up under fatigue, and you're hitting the movement milestones we set at evaluation.
02

We Move You

Therapist-guided strengthening, motor control, and movement retraining. Every rep coached.

03

You Move You

Transition to independence. Own the plan. Leave with the knowledge to keep yourself strong.

Phase 3

You Move You — independence.

Phase 3 is where most care ends too early. The pain's gone, you've got strength back — and then the plan gets handed back to you without a bridge.

Our finish is different. You leave with a written program you own, a plan for how to progress on your own, flags for what to watch for if something sneaks back, and a standing invitation to come back for a check-in without restarting the whole process.

What you leave with

A personalized maintenance program you can run yourself
Clear progression plan — when and how to increase load
Early warning signs — what to watch for, and when to call us
An open door — quick tune-ups when life happens
You're done when you've hit your goals, you understand why your body did what it did, and you have the tools to handle the next thing life throws at you.
